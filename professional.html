Clinical Supervision for LPCs

Structured, supportive supervision for LPCs working toward independent licensure in Ohio. Supervision is grounded in clinical skill development, ethical practice, and real-world application, with a focus on helping clinicians grow in both confidence and competence.

Supervision meets state requirements and includes regular individual and/or group sessions, documentation review, and guidance aligned with Ohio Counselor, Social Worker, and Marriage and Family Therapist Board standards.

Internship Opportunities

We offer internship placements for graduate-level counseling and social work students seeking hands-on experience in a supportive, clinically focused environment. Interns gain exposure to real-world practice, including assessment, treatment planning, documentation, and client engagement.

All internships are structured to meet university and state requirements, with consistent supervision, clear expectations, and opportunities to build practical skills that translate beyond the classroom.

Currently only accepting interns with CT status in Ohio.

Work With Us

Tough Cookie Counseling is always looking to connect with clinicians who value autonomy, integrity, and meaningful work.

All clinician roles with Tough Cookie Counseling are structured as 1099 independent contractor positions. This model offers flexibility and autonomy, allowing clinicians to manage their own schedules and caseloads while operating within a supportive, compliant framework.

Contractors are responsible for maintaining their own licensure, liability insurance, and tax obligations, in accordance with state and federal guidelines.

Ideal candidates are grounded, self-directed, and committed to serving first responders, families, and the broader community with intention and professionalism.

Please email us at toughcookiecounseling@gmail.com if interested in working with us.

CEU Development & Training

We design and deliver continuing education and training opportunities tailored to the real-world needs of clinicians, first responders, and organizations. Topics focus on practical application, clinical relevance, and the unique challenges faced in high-stress roles, including trauma, substance use, ethics, and family systems.

Trainings are developed to meet state CEU requirements where applicable and can be offered as individual courses, group trainings, or customized programs for agencies and teams.
