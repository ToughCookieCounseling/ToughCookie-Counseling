Frequently Asked Questions

Starting therapy often comes with practical questions. Here are answers to some of the most common things people want to know before reaching out.

Who do you work with?

Tough Cookie Counseling works with first responders, their loved ones, individuals, couples, and families seeking grounded, thoughtful mental health support.

Do you only work with first responders?

No. First responders and their families are an important area of focus, but services are also available to others who are navigating stress, trauma, relationship strain, life transitions, and emotional overload.

What services do you offer?

Services may include individual therapy, couples therapy, family support, first responder-focused care, consultation, and professional offerings such as supervision or training.

Do you offer telehealth?

If telehealth is part of your practice, you can keep this answer as: Yes. Telehealth services are available for clients in Ohio and Kentucky where clinically appropriate.

What states do you serve?

Tough Cookie Counseling is currently serving clients in Ohio and Kentucky.

Do you take insurance?

Update this answer to match your actual practice. For now, you can use: Please reach out directly for the most current information regarding insurance, private pay, and payment options.

How do I get started?

The easiest way to get started is to visit the Contact page and reach out by phone or email. From there, we can talk through next steps, availability, and whether the practice feels like the right fit for your needs.

What if I am not sure therapy is the right fit?

That is completely okay. Many people reach out with questions before committing. Starting with a conversation can help clarify what you are looking for and whether this feels like the right place to begin.

Is what I share confidential?

Therapy is confidential within the limits of the law and professional ethics. Specific policies, including exceptions to confidentiality, will be reviewed as part of the intake process.

What if I am in crisis?

Tough Cookie Counseling is not a crisis service. If you are in immediate danger or experiencing an emergency, call 911, go to your nearest emergency room, or contact 988 for immediate crisis support.

If your question is not answered here, reaching out is always welcome. Sometimes the clearest next step is simply starting the conversation.
